SALT Editorial

The Health Benefits of Coffee

Designing editorial spreads for a magazine through content development, masthead, and cover refinement.
Role: Sophomore Graphic Designer
Timeline: 10 weeks
Tools: InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop

Overview: With a randomized publication name and article, this project allowed experimentation and gave insights into the process of making and designing a magazine editorial.

My Goals

After receiving my publication name and article focusing on the food industry, I chose to make my editorial food and wellness-focused. My primary goal was to learn how to effectively work with type and layout on spreads. I also aimed to design an editorial that captures attention, communicates content effectively, and encourages readers to revisit the issue or publication.

Problems I Encountered

The initial weeks were challenging as I didn't have a clear direction for my publication. Through the class, I experimented with physical collaging and basic type placements. By weeks 6-7, I began to solidify the path for my design and layout.

Reflections

What I Learned: This project taught me a lot about typographic variations and how they can harmonize with images, elements, and the overall spread with the right balance. Additionally, I learned that exploring different media or methods can help overcome creative blocks.

Changes?: If I could, I would add more pages to my publication to further explore typographic variations and visual language. I also would like to experiment with creating a publication using my own media for a more personal touch.

Overall, I learned a great deal through this project and am highly satisfied with the final outcome. Despite its flaws, I believe I achieved the goals I set for myself at the start of this project and class.
