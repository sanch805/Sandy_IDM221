Persephone Books

Persephone Books — Graphic Design

Redesigning the logo and rebranding for Persephone Books; creating a responsive website, tablet, and mobile design based on the redesigns.
Role: Junior Graphic Designer
Timeline: 10 weeks
Tools: Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop

Overview: Persephone Books is a UK-based publishing house and bookstore that reprints neglected fiction and non-fiction, mostly by women writers, mostly dating from the mid-twentieth century. The founder, Nicola Beauman, started their journey in 1999.

The Problem

Graphic Design Aspect: Persephone Books' original logo is very simple — it's an image of Persephone — but it didn't seem iconic enough to be a recognizable mark.

UI/UX Aspect: The current website's cluttered and repetitive content, combined with a messy information hierarchy, creates a potentially overwhelming experience. The goal was to enhance the website and app's visual design while improving navigation and usability.

My Goal

For the logo redesign, I aimed to create a simple, iconic mark that could also work on a book spine. Initially, I explored combining Persephone and books but ultimately focused on Persephone alone to retain simplicity. On the website and app redesign, I aimed to simplify the information hierarchy, reduce clutter, and establish a user-friendly environment. This included grouping repetitive content into broader categories and improving the overall visual design.

Reflections

What I Learned: This project enhanced my understanding of auto-layout, components, responsive design, and the importance of style guides and variables. These tools streamlined the redesign process and made iterations easier.

Changes? If given another chance, I would explore designs that retain Persephone Books' vintage aesthetic while modernizing functionality. I appreciate the vintage charm of their current branding and would love to experiment with merging that style with my redesign concepts.

Overall, I'm proud of how this project turned out. It pushed me to think outside the box and experiment with various features and designs. While there's room for improvement, I believe my redesign effectively organizes the content and enhances usability while maintaining an aesthetically pleasing design.
